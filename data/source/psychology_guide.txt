TITLE: GUIDE TO EMOTION MANAGEMENT AND PSYCHOLOGICAL FIRST AID
CHAPTER 1: SADNESS AND DEPRESSION
Sadness is a natural reaction to loss or disappointment.

Behavioral Activation Technique: Even if you don't feel like it, performing a small action (washing a cup, walking for 5 minutes) sends a positive signal to the brain.

The 5-Minute Rule: Tell yourself you will only do the activity for 5 minutes. Often, the momentum is enough to keep you going.

Note: In cases of intense feelings of emptiness, social reconnection is crucial, even if it is passive (e.g., going to a café without speaking).

CHAPTER 2: ANXIETY AND FEAR
Anxiety is an overestimation of danger and an underestimation of our resources.

Box Breathing Technique: Inhale for 4s, Hold for 4s, Exhale for 4s, Hold for 4s. Repeat 4 times. This forces the parasympathetic system to calm the heart.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique: Name 5 things you see, 4 things you touch, 3 things you hear, 2 things you smell, and 1 thing you taste.

CHAPTER 3: ANGER AND FRUSTRATION
Anger is often a secondary emotion that hides sadness or fear. It is energy that needs to be released.

Don't react in the heat of the moment. Apply the "STOP" method:

S: Stop

T: Take a breath

O: Observe

P: Proceed

Expressive Writing: Writing down everything you feel on paper, and then tearing up the sheet, allows you to externalize your feelings without hurting others.

CHAPTER 4: JOY AND GRATITUDE
Cultivating joy strengthens mental resilience.

Gratitude Journal: Noting down 3 positive things from the day every evening trains the brain to spot the positive (Reversing the Negativity Bias).

Social Sharing of Joy (Capitalization): Sharing good news increases the intensity of the emotion for both the person sharing it and the person listening.
